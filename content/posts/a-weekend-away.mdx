The drive north from Edinburgh takes about two hours before the landscape decides to stop being polite. Then suddenly: heather, grey sky, mountains that look like they've been there since before anyone thought to name them. I pulled over three times just to stand in the wind and say nothing.

## Why the Highlands

I'd been tired in that specific way that rest doesn't fix. You know the kind — where you sleep eight hours and wake up still carrying something. A friend who'd been through the same thing six months earlier said, simply: *go somewhere with no phone signal*. The Highlands were the obvious choice.

I rented a small stone cottage outside Glencoe and arrived after dark. No neighbours visible. The kind of silence that has texture.

## Days Without an Agenda

The structure of the days surprised me. Without things to do, I did more:

1. **Day one** — walked along Loch Achtriochtan until my boots were ruined. Made soup from whatever was in the cottage. Read 200 pages of a novel I'd been carrying for a year.
2. **Day two** — drove the A82 north to Glencoe village. Ate a pie at a pub that had been a pub since 1770. Drove back the long way, through fog.
3. **Day three** — did almost nothing. Sat outside with coffee until the midges found me. Packed slowly.

## What the Highlands Are Actually Like

Cold in September, even on sunny days. The light changes constantly — you can watch four different weathers happen to the same mountain in a single afternoon. Wildlife: red deer on the road at dusk, something that might have been an osprey, one very confident highland cow who refused to move for my car.

> The Highlands don't ask anything of you. That's the point.

## The Thing About Quiet

I hadn't realised how loud my life had gotten until I stood on the shore of Loch Linnhe at 7am with no one else around. The water was absolutely still. I heard a fish jump. I heard my own breathing.

That's what I came back with, more than anything — not photographs, not a list of restaurants. Just the memory of what it felt like to be somewhere that didn't need me to perform anything.
